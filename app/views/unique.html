Designing the Prototype

To break the constrains stated above, we designed an armband which measures electrical activity from people's muscles to identify five basic gestures from their hands. With the help of EMG sensors, we are able to collect the biological data from muscles when people make gestures. Then we trans- form the data into digital signal. After extracting and processing the characteristic information through our algorithms, gestures can be detected.

The OneArm armband mainly consists of operational amplifier, instrumentation amplifier, power management integrated circuit, and microcontroller.

We 3D printed the cases that contain eletrical components and connect them together using springs to be flexible enough to fit in different sizes of arms.

Application Client

To let users update the real-time status about their OneArms, make personal settings and interact with compatible applications and devices, we designed the application client. With the client, people are able to switch the device or application controlled to another. We defined five basic gestures for users to operate: fist, double tap, spread fingers, wave right and wave left. which are native and intuitive actions that people make to control the things.

User Guide

People will access to the necessary instruction on how to use OneArm. When user tries to make five different basic gestures in user Guide, software's standard model will be calibrated to user's personal data so that our algorithms could detect the gestures quicker.

OneArm Manager

With the manager, users can use gestures to switch applications they currently control to another, making the non-mouse experience on computer possible. They can also view or manage the status of their OneArms in Setting.

What Are the Differences from Myo?

The idea of OneArm emerged nearly at the same time with Myo. Lin Zhao, the founder of OneArm, took the original idea of OneArm to participate in Imagine Cup held by Microsoft in April, 2013. OneArm got in the finals and was rewared as the second place in China Division. Meanwhile, the demo video of Myo first came out and greatly aroused people's interest. After Lin Zhao graduated in June, 2014, he decided to start up his own company and turn OneArm into real commercial product.

The principle of two products are basically the same; both utilized sEMG to detect people's gestures. Myo has already gained a number of customers and the API is also open to developers, which encourages more applications developed for Myo. OneArm currently is in the phase of demo and looking for Venture Capital.

Our vision is to build OneArm for special demands from China's users, cutting in China's market. Thus, we have less complex functions than that of Myo. Myo's market price is 1,600RMB - 2,000RMB. We are confident that the cost of OneArm could be reduced to around 200RMB when it goes into production.

Personal Thoughts

In March, 2015, I was invited by Lin Zhao to join the team as the part-time Design Lead at OneArm. This was my first startup's experience. It was totally different from any projects I have done before: I needed to consider the demands for real-world users, be involved with other team members to fix the problems, learn to illustrate the idea to investors clearly and many other things that I learned a lot from.

In May, 2015, after joined the team for nearly three months, we eventually launched our first demo. We demonstrated OneArm in several entrepreneurial forums and met a lot of investors, some of which showed interest in funding us_ Although I did not continue to work with the team until OneArm goes into production and gets into the market for customers, I really appreciated the time I spent with them on working hard to build the product. I have gained lots of valuable experiences about startups and entrepreneurship, which better prepared me for future career with a wider vision.
